Aakruthi

ECE Department Osmania College of Engineering / Technical Fest

Forever the forerunner in imparting quality education, the department of Electronics and Communication Engineering has been persistent and consistent in catering to the best needs of the industry. In keeping up this spirit, to foster creativity and innovation, a group of students from this department with the support and guidance of the faculty, have initiated the Technical Symposium Aakruthi in 2000. Thanks to their bold initiative and unparalleled efforts, this event is being organized year after year since then, upholding their vision and aspirations.

The event�s pivotal motive has been to encourage students to confront practical problems and mobilize their skills towards devising an optimal solution. The event undoubtedly has served as a platform where students can explore themselves and set a righteous path for their bright careers.

The event has been eliciting an overwhelming response from students from different parts of A.P. and also other states. Last year around 150 papers and projects have been received and over 450 students have participated. The students' organizing committees have been striving to improve the fest in terms of the number of events, better organization and hospitality and wider scope to showcase one�s abilities.

Achievements

  • SSS PROJECT

    The Sustainability Support Scheme (SSS) Programe of the Ministry of Information Technology, Government of India, took part. The main objectives of this Project were to make the department self-sustainable through earning by way of conducting (Continuing Engineering Education Programe) CEEP courses, consultancy etc...

  • TEQUIP Project

    Under Technical Education Quality Improvement Programme (TEQUIP) many new Laboratories like VLSI, Advanced Communication Systems, Digital Signal Processing Speech Signal Processing are established.

  • SONET Program

    The Department has also contributed learning modules in the SONET Program in the areas of Communication Engineering, DSP and Network Theory.
